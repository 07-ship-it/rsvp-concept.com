The Story

Borrowed from a Manhattan that never quite let the lights go out

It opened quietly, in November 2014, modeled on the hidden bars and supper rooms of prohibition-era New York — low light, brass fittings, the sense that you needed to know someone to get a seat.

A year later came The Poolside: a second room entirely, open to the sky and built for a different hour of the night. Two venues, one address, two very different moods.

What to Order

The dishes guests keep coming back for

Salmon Fillet

The dish that comes up again and again — more than one guest has called it the best salmon they've had in the city, cooked through with a silky, even finish.

Prawn & Calamari

A standing favorite for the table to share — the dish regulars point newcomers toward before anything else.

Squid Starter

A smaller plate that wins outsized praise, often mentioned alongside the grouper as the pair to order together.

Steamed Grouper

Light, well-seasoned, and consistently singled out by guests looking for something other than the obvious order.

House note — steaks here run done through medium; rare and medium-rare aren't really on offer.

After Dark

The Poolside

Behind the main dining room sits a second world: an open-air bar and lounge that opened a year after RSVP itself, built for nights when the ceiling should be the sky instead of a roof.

Some evenings bring a live band, others a DJ — and at least one guest arrived early on a Friday to find the place nearly empty and the jazz already setting the mood.
